I'm a graduate student at Northwestern University studying programming languages. Right now, I'm working on making JavaScript and TypeScript more pleasant to write. Challenges working with those languages at scale abound, and I'm interested in making the web a simpler place.

My favorite language is LISP, and I want some dialect of it to spread. I want as many people as possible to discover the joy of building programs by writing down the syntax tree that represents them directly. If you're interested in learning more, consider picking up Racket.

I enjoy untangling dirty code and messy systems. Cleaning up technical debt may not seem glamarous, but I enjoy making the world more maintainable one program at a time.

Other interests include:

  • Web development

Before becoming a developer, I wanted to work as a high-school English teacher. I'm a big fan of Russian literature, English education, and musical theatre.
